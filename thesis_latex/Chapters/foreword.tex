This thesis was carried out at the Future Computing Group, CSE Research Unit,
Faculty of Information Technology and Electrical Engineering, University of
Oulu, in collaboration with TalentAdore Ltd. The work was supervised
academically by Lauri Lov\'en, with industrial supervision from Asim Nadeem
and Oskari Valkama at TalentAdore.

I owe my warmest thanks to Lauri for the latitude to scope a project around
an unfamiliar empirical question --- the way coordination under compression
behaves at institutional scale --- and for the discipline he brought to
distinguishing the master's-scale deliverable from the doctoral-scale work it
will eventually feed. I am grateful to Asim Nadeem and Oskari Valkama for
sharing TalentAdore's production cost structure, which anchored the
cost-per-workflow arm of the evaluation in a real industrial setting. The
work on agent-readiness for the real-trace transfer arm was conducted in
parallel with Mohammad Abaeiani (publication database agent, M0), Faisal
Khan (contract database agent, M1), and Vu Truong (Moodle and Patio harness,
M2); I thank them for their willingness to coordinate connector schedules
around the demands of this thesis.

The thesis was prepared on a single Apple M4 Pro 48~GB workstation. All
training, evaluation, and inference fit within that envelope, including the
70B int4 single-point characterisation that closes the model-size scaling
arm. The reproducibility package and the open-source reference implementation
accompany this manuscript.
